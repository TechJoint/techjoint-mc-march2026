Trigger Link Campaigns — Automatic Audience Filtering

Send one email to your full list. Based on which link each person clicks, they automatically get sorted into different follow-up sequences. Build it once, and it runs on autopilot all year.

How to set this up:

  1. Go to Marketing → Emails and create your campaign. In the email body, add links for each offer or topic (e.g., "View Catering Menu," "Book a Party," "See Weekly Specials"). Under each link's settings, check "Enable Trigger Link" and name the trigger.
  2. Go to Automation → Workflows and create a new workflow. Set the trigger to "Trigger Link Clicked" and select the link name you created. Add follow-up actions — send a specific email sequence, apply a tag, notify your team, or move them into a pipeline stage.
  3. Repeat for each trigger link. Now every link in your original email routes people into a tailored follow-up — no manual sorting, no extra sends, no extra cost until someone actually engages.

Bulk SMS to All Contacts

Send promotions, announcements, or reminders to your entire list or a filtered segment in one step. Responses come back into the CRM conversation thread, so you can follow up individually.

How to set this up:

  1. Go to Contacts → Smart Lists and either select "All Contacts" or create a filtered list (e.g., contacts tagged "Catering Leads" or contacts who haven't visited in 30 days). This is your audience.
  2. Go to Marketing → Campaigns, create a new campaign, and choose SMS as the channel. Write your message — keep it under 160 characters for a single segment. Use personalization fields like {{contact.first_name}} to make it feel direct.
  3. Select your smart list as the recipient group, set the send time (or send immediately), and launch. Every reply lands in Conversations so you or your team can respond in real time.

AI-Generated Blog Content

Use AI to draft full blog posts based on a topic or keyword. Edit as needed, publish to your site, and build organic search presence over time — no writing experience required.

How to set this up:

  1. Go to Sites → Blogs and click "Create Post." Enter your topic or keyword (e.g., "best catering ideas for corporate events"). Click the AI Content button to generate a full draft — title, body, and meta description.
  2. Review and edit the draft. Add your own details, swap in real examples, and adjust the tone. Set a featured image and add relevant tags or categories for your site's blog section.
  3. Hit Publish (or schedule for later). The post goes live on your website automatically. Over time, these posts build search visibility and bring in organic traffic — people finding your business through Google without paying for ads.
